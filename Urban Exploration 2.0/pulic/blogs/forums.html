1. Where to Connect with Like-Minded Explorers

The urbex (urban exploration) community is a network of like-minded individuals who share a passion for exploring abandoned and forgotten places.

Online Forums and Social Media: Urbex forums and social media groups are excellent places to connect with others who share your interest. Websites like Reddit (r/urbanexploration) and forums like UER (Urban Exploration Resource) provide spaces to discuss experiences, share photos, and exchange tips.

Instagram: Instagram is a popular platform for urbex photographers to showcase their work and connect with a wider audience. Search for urbex-related hashtags like #urbex, #abandoned, or #decay to discover fellow explorers and their work.

Facebook Groups: Many urbex communities have dedicated Facebook groups where members can share their experiences, post photos, and plan outings. Search for urbex-related groups in your region or area of interest.

Flickr and 500px: Flickr and 500px are photography-focused platforms where you can find a wealth of urbex photography. Connect with photographers, share your work, and participate in discussions.

Urbex Websites and Blogs: Explore urbex websites and blogs for articles, photo galleries, and resources. Some websites also offer forums and opportunities to submit your own content.

2. Joining the Conversation

UER (Urban Exploration Resource): Website: UER.ca UER is one of the largest and most active urbex communities on the internet. It offers a wealth of resources, including forums, location databases, trip reports, and photography galleries. You can connect with explorers from around the world and access a treasure trove of information on urbex sites.

Reddit - r/urbanexploration:Subreddit: r/urbanexploration Reddit's urbex community is a hub for discussions, photo sharing, and advice. You can ask questions, share your explorations, and engage in conversations with fellow enthusiasts. The subreddit has a vibrant and active user base.

28DaysLater:Website: 28dayslater.co.uk 28DaysLater is a UK-based urbex forum, but it also includes sections for international exploration. It's known for its detailed trip reports, location discussions, and a supportive community of urban explorers.

DarkPlaces: Website: DarkPlaces.org DarkPlaces is a European-focused urbex forum with a strong emphasis on photography. It's a great place to connect with explorers in Europe and beyond, share your photography, and discuss locations.

Infiltration - The Zine About Going Places You're Not Supposed to Go: Website: Infiltration.org Infiltration is both an online resource and a physical zine dedicated to urban exploration. The website features articles, stories, and discussions related to urbex and urban adventure.

Facebook Groups: There are numerous urbex-related Facebook groups, both global and region-specific. Search for groups that align with your interests, and you can connect with explorers in your area or share your experiences with a broader audience.

3. Stories of Collaboration and Support

Collaborative Documentation: Urbex photographers often collaborate with fellow explorers they meet through online communities. These collaborations can result in stunning photo series and videos that showcase the beauty and history of abandoned places. Explorers combine their skills to capture unique angles, lighting, and perspectives, producing captivating visual narratives.

Discovering Hidden Gems: Online urbex forums and social media groups allow members to share their discoveries. These platforms serve as digital treasure troves of hidden and forgotten places. One explorer's post about an intriguing location can lead to others visiting and documenting it, preserving its history through photography and stories.

Safety and Information Sharing: Urbex communities prioritize safety. Members often share vital information about access points, hazards, and the condition of locations. This information helps ensure that explorers are well-prepared and aware of potential risks before venturing into unknown territory.

Mentorship and Learning: Seasoned urban explorers often mentor newcomers through online communities. They provide guidance on best practices, equipment recommendations, and tips for respectful exploration. This mentorship fosters a culture of responsible urbex and helps newcomers learn the ropes.

Preserving History: Urbex communities are passionate about preserving the history of abandoned places. Members collaborate to research the backgrounds of locations, uncovering stories and historical context that might otherwise be lost to time. This dedication to preserving history enriches the exploration experience.

4. The Benefits of Joining Urbex Groups

Collective Knowledge:Urbex groups often consist of members with varying levels of experience and expertise. When you explore as a group, you can tap into the collective knowledge of your fellow members. They may know about access points, potential hazards, or the history of a location, which can enhance your overall experience and safety.

Risk Mitigation: Exploring abandoned places can be inherently risky. When you're in a group, there's a better chance of quickly addressing any unexpected situations or emergencies. Multiple sets of eyes can spot potential dangers, and group members can assist one another in navigating challenging terrain or obstacles.

Equipment Sharing: Group members often bring a variety of equipment, such as flashlights, first-aid kits, and communication devices. This shared equipment can be valuable in case of emergencies or equipment failures.

Emotional Support: Urbex can be emotionally intense, especially in locations with a haunting or eerie atmosphere. Being part of a group provides emotional support and camaraderie. You can share your feelings and experiences with people who understand and empathize with your emotions.

Splitting Tasks: Exploring in a group allows you to divide tasks and responsibilities. One member can focus on navigation, another on documenting the location, and another on safety protocols. This division of labor ensures that all aspects of the exploration are covered.

5. How to Connect with Local Explorers

Join Online Urbex Communities:Online urbex forums, social media groups, and websites are excellent places to connect with local explorers. Participate in discussions, share your experiences, and ask for recommendations on groups or individuals to explore with.

Attend Urbex Meetups and Events: Keep an eye out for urbex meetups, conventions, or events happening in your region. These gatherings offer opportunities to meet fellow explorers, share stories, and plan future adventures.

Use Social Media:Platforms like Instagram, Facebook, and Twitter are popular among urbex enthusiasts. Search for local urbex groups, pages, or profiles and start engaging with their content. You can also use location-based hashtags to discover explorers in your area.

Explore Urbex Hotspots: Visit well-known urbex locations in your area during peak exploration times. You're likely to encounter other explorers at these spots, and striking up a conversation can lead to new friendships.

Engage in Urbex Photography:If you're a photographer, consider posting your urbex photos online, especially on photography platforms like Flickr, 500px, or photography-focused urbex forums. Fellow photographers may reach out to collaborate or explore together.

6. Navigating the World of Online Content Creators

Seek Ethical and Responsible Content: Look for urbex bloggers and vloggers who prioritize safety, ethics, and respect for property rights. Responsible explorers will often mention their commitment to these principles in their content.

Research the Content Creator: Take the time to learn about the content creator's background, experience, and motivations for urban exploration. This information can provide context for their content and help you gauge their level of expertise.

Explore a Variety of Perspectives: Urbex is a diverse hobby with explorers from various backgrounds and approaches. Explore content from different creators to gain a well-rounded understanding of urbex in different regions and styles.

Engage in Discussions: Leave thoughtful comments on blog posts or video comments to engage with content creators and fellow viewers. Meaningful discussions can lead to valuable insights and connections within the urbex community.

Verify Location Information:If a content creator shares the locations of their explorations, use this information responsibly. Always verify the accuracy and current status of locations before attempting to visit them. Avoid disclosing sensitive or dangerous details online.
